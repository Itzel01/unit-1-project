Immigration: a timeline

  • 1808: Congress outlawed the slave trade but slaves were still being transported illegally until the year 1850.
  • 1820: 1/3 of all immigrants are Irish. They are catholic and they are settling in Port cities. 5 million Germans settled in the Midwest.
  • 1850: Steady flow of Chinese workers immigrated. They came because the Opium Wars left a lot of them unemployed and the Californian Gold Rush was an opportunity for them to have a better life.
  • 1880-1920: More than 20 Million immigrants arrived. 4 Million of new arrivals were Italian and 2 Million were Jewish.
  • 1892: Ellis Island opened in January and more than 12 Million immigrants entered.
  • 1910: About 3 quarters of New York's popultion consists of new immigrants and first generation Americans.
  • 1924: Illegal immigration increases. Early border crossers were Asian because they were barred from entering.
  • 1956-1957: 38,000 Immigrants came from Hungary becasue of their failed attempt to uprise against the Soviet Union. They were the first Cold War refugees. Another 3 million people entered because of the Cold War.
  • 1960-1962: 14,000 children entered due to Operation Peter Pan.
  • 1980: Marial Boatlift. 125,000 cuban refugees arrived at Florida shore seeking political asylum.

Immigration Acts: a timeline

  • 1790: Nationality Act of 1790. First Law that defined citizenship. It created standards and procedures by which immigrants could become citizens. Limited to only free white persons. This law created the category of aliens that are ineligible for citizenship.
  • 1791-1804 : Congressed banned the immigration of free black men in fear that an uprising would occur like in Haiti.
  • 1798: Alien and Sedation Acts of 1798. The first deportation laws. They were not enforced and the Adams administration faced critique for the harsh laws. The Acts stated that people who posed a political threat could be detained and deported.
    Alien Enemies law of 1798 allowed the president to detain, deport or relocate immigrants from hostile countries in time of war.
  • 1819: Steerage Act. This act was created because of the horrific traveling conditions on ships. This act required captains to have better conditions and it was also required captains to submit demographic information for every passanger onboard.
  • 1830: Indians are forced off of their lands.
  • 1848: Major part of Northern Mexico is annexed and now a part of the United States.
  • 1849 - 1860: Know Nothings. This was a group that often harrassed immigrants and spread political propaganda against them.
  • 1882: Chinese Exclusion Act. Anti-Chinese sentiment grew because their sucess was growing. White workiners blamed them for low wages. The Chinese Exclusion Act was the first to place broad restrictions on certain immigration groups.
  • 1891: Immigration Act of 1891. The Immigration Act of 1891 excludes who can enter. Polygamist, convicts and people whom were sick were among the few that were denied entrance. The Act also created an office of immigration.
  • 1907: Gentlemen's Agreement. This agreement was created because a lot of Japanese people were entering the United States. The agreement was that Japan was going to limit emigration and the president Theodore Rooselvelt was going to urge San Francisco to end segregation in schools.
  • 1917: Immigration Act of 1917. This Act created a literacy requirement for immigrants entering the country. This Act halted immigration from most Asian countries. The phillipines was the only Asian country that immigrants were allowed to pass from. This was mostly because it was a United States territory.
  • 1924: Immigration Act of 1924. This Act limited the number of immigrants entering from foreign countries through a yearly quota. The United States issued visas to 2% of the people in each nationality. This quota was biased and allowed a lot of white people to enter the United States legally. This Act Completely left out Asian immigrants. The United States Border Patrol was established to stop any Asians attempting to cross from the Mexican and Canadian borders.
  • 1942: Bracero Program. Mexican Agricultural Workers were allowed to enter the United States because of the labor shortages due to Word War II. The program lasted until 1964.
  • 1948: First Refugee and Resettlement law to deal with influx of European seeking permanent residence in the U.S. after World War II.
  • 1952: McCarren - Walter Act fomally ended the exclusion of Asian immigration to the U.S.
  • 1960-1962: Operation Peter Pan. 125,000 children were transported to the United States, unaccompanied. The Operation was part of the United States' anti-communist agenda.
  • 1965: Immigration and Nationality Act. The quota system that was in place was ended by president Lyndon B Johnson.
  • 1970: Naturalization Act. Allowed any free white person who had been living in the United States for 2 or more years to apply for citizenship. The census was created this year.
  • 1986: Simpson-Mazzoli Act. This Act granted amnesty to more than 3 million immigrants living illegally in the United States.
  • 2001: U.S. Senators proposed the Dream Act. Dream Act would provide a pathway towards legal status for those who qualify. The Dream Act was not approved.
  • 2012: U.S. President Barack Obama passes DACA. DACA is not a pathway to citizenship and is mostly to halt deportation for those applicable.
  • 2017: Donald Trump placed a travel ban aimed at Chad, Iran, Libya, Syria, Yemen, Somalia, North Korea, and Venezuela. He called it "Protecting the Nation from Foreign Terrorist". He also ended DACA.
  • 2018: Chad is lifted from the Travel Ban but the other countries aren't.
