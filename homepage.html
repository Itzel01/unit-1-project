Mission Statement

Our mission is to provide the information that an undocumented person may need. Provide them with organizations that could help them get citizenship or even defend them if they ever need it. Our mission is to educate people who may not know what it is like to be undocumented so they won't judge people before they have even met them.

Literal Definition

An Undocumented Immigrant is a person who is foreign born and has no legal right to be or remain in the United States. Some misconceptions are that Undocumented Immigrants all crossed the border. This is not true. There is a large number of people who cross the border but there is also a large number of tourist and travellers who have simply overstayed their visa or their visa expired.

Emotional Baggage

There is so much emotional baggage that comes with being undocumented. There are people who live in constant fear of deportation. People who used to be so proud of being bilingual and proud of their culture are now terrified to even speak their native tongues. This is due to the huge amount of harrassment that they have experienced throughout the years of living in the United States. They are so used to being told that they aren't enough that they actually begin to believe it. There is the homesickness that comes everytime you call your family and they ask how are you. You may want to cry and go home but you can't because you worked so hard to even get here, worked to live in the States, in search of the American Dream.

A day in their life...

A day in the life of an undocumented person may look exactly like the day of a documented person except their job probably pays them less than minimum wage and they will avoid interactions with authority at all costs. SO actually not at all like the day of a documented person. The reason why they accept the less than minimum wage is because their emplyers may take advantage of their status. An Undocumneted person may just feel Lucky to have even been hired. They fear authority because there is already so much racial bias and if they get caught being undocumented, there is a chance that they may even be deported. Deportation is something that many people fear and avoid at all costs.
